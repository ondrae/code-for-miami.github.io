Civic Hacking 101

Get Involved

What is civic hacking?

Civic hacking is when engaged citizens come together to improve their cities via technology, innovation and design. Anyone with the drive to improve where they live and the will to collaborate with others to solve urban problems can be a civic hacker.

Across the country, civic hackers have created local wikis to share knowledge about their city,developed an app from government data to help citizens locate local flu shot providers, and pioneered a platform to help residents in need pay their water bills.

At Code for Miami, we have several projects underway to make the lives of Miamians better. Check them out, lend a hand and become a civic hacker today.

What is open data?

Open data is data that is available for anyone and everyone to use and redistribute. While the data may exist in various forms (documents, databases, records or transcripts, among others), it is open if it meets the following conditions:

  1. It must be accessible and available for download in a convenient and complete form.
  2. It must be available for re-use, redistribution and mixing with other data sets.
  3. It must be universally available for anyone and everyone to use for any purpose, commercial or otherwise.

Principles

  1. Start with people's needs
  2. Ensure everyone can participate
  3. Start small and continuously improve
  4. Use real-time data to inform decisions
  5. Default to open
  6. Build the right text-transform
  7. Make informed decisions

Who can contribute?

Anyone with the drive to improve our community and the will to collaborate with others can contribute to Code for Miami.

We welcome coders, writers, designers, idea-makers, leaders, followers, project managers, sponsors, entrepreneurs, restaurateurs, donors and anyone willing to contribute something to making our city better.

How can you contribute?

The possibilities for contributing to Code for Miami are endless, but here are some ways to get started:

  • Come out to our weekly Civic Hack Night at the LAB, happening every Monday from 7 to 9 p.m., to contribute to one of our projects.
  • Attend one of our upcoming events or workshops.
  • Partner with us to develop an event or sponsor a planned event.
  • Feed our civic hackers by donating food to our weekly meetings.
  • If any of the above options entice you, or if you have an idea for another way to contribute, let us know how you want to get involved.
